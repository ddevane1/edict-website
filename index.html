About EDICT

Making clinical trials work for everyone

Clinical trials shape modern medicine. That is why it is crucial to ensure that no population is left out. Unfortunately, a lack of diversity in trial participants risks skewed data and inequitable outcomes.

EDICT establishes an innovative Doctoral Network to transform how equity, diversity, and inclusion (EDI) principles are embedded in clinical trials across Europe. Through a dynamic doctoral network, EDICT will train researchers to redesign how trials are planned, conducted, and assessed.

Drawing on expertise from academia, industry, patient advocacy, and policy, the project plans to deliver practical tools‚Äîfrom bias-detecting AI to inclusive consent processes‚Äîcreating a future in which clinical trials truly reflect the populations they aim to serve.

Research Domains

Four Pillars of Transformation

Our doctoral candidates will develop comprehensive expertise across interconnected research domains.

01

Measuring Diversity

Developing standardised metrics and frameworks for assessing and reporting diversity in clinical trial populations.

02

Innovative Methodologies

Creating new EDI methodologies including AI-powered bias detection and culturally-responsive research approaches.

03

Implementation Tools

Building practical tools for inclusive recruitment, accessible consent processes, and representative data collection.

04

Systemic Change

Developing frameworks for embedding EDI principles into regulatory guidance, industry practice, and research culture.
