Join the EDICT Network

We are recruiting 16 exceptional doctoral candidates to transform equity, diversity and inclusion in clinical trials across Europe. All positions are fully funded with competitive salaries and benefits.

Overview

EDICT (Advancing Equity, Diversity and Inclusion in Clinical Trials) is a Marie SkÅ‚odowska-Curie Doctoral Network funded by the European Union's Horizon Europe programme. We are offering 16 fully-funded PhD positions across 6 European countries, with opportunities for international secondments to academic, industry, and non-profit partners.

Each doctoral candidate will be enrolled in a world-class PhD programme while contributing to cutting-edge research that will shape the future of inclusive clinical trials.

Eligibility Requirements

All applicants must meet the Marie SkÅ‚odowska-Curie Actions (MSCA) eligibility criteria:

Mobility Rule: At the time of recruitment (Sept 2026), candidates must not have resided or carried out their main activity (work, study, etc.) in the country of their host institution for more than 12 months in the 36 months immediately prior to recruitment. Short stays such as holidays are not counted.

Degree Requirements: Candidates must hold a degree that formally qualifies them to embark on a doctorate. Requirements vary by position but typically include:

  • A Master's degree (or equivalent) in a relevant discipline
  • A strong academic record, typically at least a 2.1 honours degree (or international equivalent) at undergraduate level
  • Specific skills or experience as outlined in individual project descriptions

Language Requirements: Excellent written and spoken English is required for all positions. Some positions may benefit from additional language skills.

Early-Stage Researcher: Candidates must be in the first four years of their research career and not yet have been awarded a doctoral degree.

How to Apply

Applications will open 2nd March 2026 for positions starting in September 2026. The application process will include:

  1. Online Application: Submit your application through our online portal (link will be available when applications open)
  2. Required Documents:
    • Curriculum Vitae (CV)
    • Cover letter explaining your motivation and fit for your chosen project(s)
    • Academic transcripts from undergraduate and postgraduate studies
    • Proof of English proficiency (if applicable)
    • Two academic reference letters
    • Copy of passport or ID
  3. Project Selection: You may apply for up to 3 projects in order of preference
  4. Shortlisting: Applications will be reviewed by the selection committee
  5. Interviews: Shortlisted candidates will be invited for online interviews
  6. Offers: Successful candidates will receive formal offers with contract details

Selection Criteria

Applications will be evaluated based on:

  • Academic Excellence: Quality of academic record and relevant qualifications
  • Research Potential: Evidence of research skills, critical thinking, and scientific curiosity
  • Motivation: Clear articulation of interest in the project and commitment to EDI in clinical trials
  • Relevant Experience: Prior experience in research, clinical trials, or related fields
  • Communication Skills: Ability to communicate effectively in written and spoken English
  • International Outlook: Willingness to undertake international mobility and work in diverse teams

EDICT is committed to equity, diversity and inclusion in our own recruitment. We strongly encourage applications from candidates of all backgrounds and will ensure fair and unbiased selection processes.
